# Shri Krishna Ashtakam

**Deity**: Lord Krishna
**Author**: Adi Shankaracharya
**Theme**: Devotion, Beauty, and the Play of Consciousness

---

## Verse 1

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**भजे व्रजैकमण्डनं समस्तपापखण्डनं**
**स्वभक्तचित्तरञ्जनं सदैव नन्दनन्दनम् ।**
**सुपिच्छगुच्छमस्तकं सुनादवेणुहस्तकं**
**अनङ्गरङ्गसागरं नमामि कृष्णनागरम् ॥१॥**

_Bhaje vrajaika-maṇḍanaṁ samasta-pāpa-khaṇḍanaṁ_
_Svabhakta-citta-rañjanaṁ sadaiva nanda-nandanam |_
_Supiccha-guccha-mastakaṁ sunāda-veṇu-hastakaṁ_
_Anaṅga-raṅga-sāgaraṁ namāmi Kṛṣṇa-nāgaram ||1||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Bhaje**: I worship.
- **Vraja-eka-maṇḍanaṁ**: The one (unique) ornament of Vraja (Brindavan).
- **Samasta-pāpa-khaṇḍanaṁ**: The destroyer of all sins.
- **Svabhakta-citta-rañjanaṁ**: The delighter of the minds of His own devotees.
- **Sadaiva**: Always.
- **Nanda-nandanam**: The son of Nanda (Joy of Nanda).
- **Supiccha-guccha-mastakaṁ**: Whose head is adorned with a bunch of beautiful peacock feathers.
- **Sunāda-veṇu-hastakaṁ**: Holding a flute that produces sweet/beautiful sound in His hand.
- **Anaṅga-raṅga-sāgaraṁ**: The ocean of the music/colors of love (Kamadeva).
- **Namāmi**: I bow/salute.
- **Kṛṣṇa-nāgaram**: Krishna, the gallant lover/hero.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I worship the one supreme ornament of Vraja, who destroys all sins and delights the minds of His devotees. He is always the joy of Nanda. He wears a bunch of beautiful peacock feathers on His head and holds a melodious flute in His hand. I bow to Lord Krishna, the charming hero who is an ocean of the arts of love.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं व्रज के एकमात्र आभूषण, समस्त पापों का खंडन (नाश) करने वाले, अपने भक्तों के चित्त को आनंदित करने वाले और नित्य नंदलाल (नन्द के पुत्र) को भजता हूँ। जिनके मस्तक पर सुंदर मोरपंख का गुच्छा है, जिनके हाथ में मधुर नाद करने वाली वेणु (बांसुरी) है, और जो कामदेव की कलाओं (प्रेम-रंग) के सागर हैं; ऐसे नटवर नागर श्री कृष्ण को मैं नमन करता हूँ।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Joy** and **Aesethic Sensitivity**.

- **Citta-ranjanam**: The mind (Citta) often runs after worldly pleasures for entertainment. This verse trains the mind to find its delight (_Ranjanam_) in the Divine Form instead of cheap distractions.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Peacock Feather (Piccha)**: Represents the "many eyes" of the universe or the transmutation of poison (as peacocks eat snakes) into beauty.
- **Flute (Venu)**: The human personality. It needs to be hollow (egoless) for the Divine Breath to play the music of life through it.
- **Ananga (Love)**: Krishna is the source of all attraction.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Ananda Swarupa**: The Self is pure Bliss.

- **Vraja-eka-mandanam**: In the field of the mind (Vraja), the Atman (Self) is the only true "ornament" or value. Everything else is secondary.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize your spine as the flute. Feel the breath of life flowing through it as a song played by Krishna. If the song is jarring, check if "ego" (blockage) is stuck in the bamboo.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: The belief that happiness comes from external objects.
- **Truth Installed**: The Source of all Joy (Nanda-nandana) is the Self within.

### 10) Essence

Hollow out your ego like a flute, and He will play the music of bliss.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Is my mind being "entertained" by the world or "delighted" by the Divine?
2. Am I hollow enough (open) to let life settle into a melody?
3. What "sins" (heavy emotions) do I need him to "Khandanam" (destroy) today?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Hollow Bamboo"**: Sit for 5 mins. Breathe deep. Imagine you are a hollow bamboo reed with no thoughts (knots) inside. Let the breath flow as His music.

## Verse 2

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**मनोजगर्वमोचनं विशाललोललोचनं**
**विधूतगोपशोचनं नमामि पद्मलोचनम् ।**
**करारविन्दभूधरं स्मितावलोकसुन्दरं**
**महेन्द्रमानदारणं नमामि कृष्णवारणम् ॥२॥**

_Manoja-garva-mocanaṁ viśāla-lola-locanaṁ_
_Vidhūta-gopa-śocanaṁ namāmi padma-locanam |_
_Karāravinda-bhūdharaṁ smitāvaloka-sundaraṁ_
_Mahendra-māna-dāraṇaṁ namāmi Kṛṣṇa-vāraṇam ||2||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Manoja-garva-mocanaṁ**: Liberator/Destroyer of the pride of Cupid (Manoja - born of mind).
- **Viśāla-lola-locanaṁ**: Large, unsteady (playful) eyes.
- **Vidhūta-gopa-śocanaṁ**: Destroyer of the sorrows of the Gopis/Cowherds.
- **Namāmi Padma-locanam**: I bow to the Lotus-eyed one.
- **Kara-aravinda-bhūdharaṁ**: Lifting the mountain (Govardhana) with His lotus hands.
- **Smita-avaloka-sundaraṁ**: Beautiful with a smiling glance.
- **Mahendra-māna-dāraṇaṁ**: Destroyer of the pride of Indra (Mahendra).
- **Namāmi Kṛṣṇa-vāraṇam**: I desire/bow to Krishna, who is like a wild elephant (Varana) or who wards off obstacles.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to the Lotus-eyed One, who destroys the pride of Cupid, possesses large and playful eyes, and removes the sorrows of the cowherds. He lifted the Govardhana mountain with His lotus hands and looks beautiful with a smiling glance. He crushed the pride of Indra. I bow to Krishna, who is powerful like a mad elephant.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं कमल नयन श्री कृष्ण को नमन करता हूँ, जो कामदेव का गर्व छुड़ाने वाले हैं, जिनके नेत्र विशाल और चंचल हैं, और जो गोप-गोपियों के शोक को दूर करने वाले हैं। जिन्होंने अपने कर-कमलों पर गोवर्धन पर्वत धारण किया था, जो अपनी मुस्कुराती चितवन से सुंदर लगते हैं, और जिन्होंने इंद्र (महेंद्र) के अभिमान का नाश किया। मैं उस 'कृष्ण-वारण' (गजराज समान शक्तिशाली/विघ्न निवारक) को नमन करता हूँ।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Confidence** and **Emotional Stability**.

- **Manoja-garva-mocanam**: Beauty that beats Cupid. This means overcoming lust through higher aesthetic absorption (Sublimation).
- **Indra-mana-daranam**: Crushing intellectual arrogance.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Govardhana (Mountain)**: Represents the heavy burden of Karma or Life. Krishna lifts it "with a lotus hand" (effortlessly).
- **Mad Elephant (Varanam)**: Represents Uncontrollable Power. The Divine is sweet but also wild and untamable.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Sthita-Prajna (Steady Wisdom)**:

- While his eyes are _Lola_ (playful/moving), his Act is _Bhudharam_ (steady as a rock). The realized being plays in the world but remains unshakable in Truth.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize your biggest problem as a huge mountain falling on you. See Krishna lifting it with one finger effectively shielding you. Feel the weight lift off your shoulders.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I have to carry the weight of the world alone."
- **Truth Installed**: The Divine Support (Anugraha) is available if you surrender the pride of doership.

### 10) Essence

Surrender your burden; He lifts mountains with a smile.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. What mountain (worry) am I trying to lift with my own weak hands?
2. Does my pride lead to stress (like Indra)?
3. Can I replace lust (Manoja) with love for the Divine?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Lift"**: When stressed, physically shrug your shoulders up and drop them, exhaling sharply. Say, "You lift it, Krishna."

## Verse 3

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**कदम्बसूनकुण्डलं सुचारुगण्डमण्डलं**
**व्रजाङ्गनैकवल्लभं नमामि कृष्णदुर्लभम् ।**
**यशोदया समोदया सगोपया सनन्दया**
**युतं सुखैकदायकं नमामि गोपनायकम् ॥३॥**

_Kadamba-sūna-kuṇḍalaṁ sucāru-gaṇḍa-maṇḍalaṁ_
_Vrajāṅganai-kavallabhaṁ namāmi Kṛṣṇa-durlabham |_
_Yaśodayā samodayā sagopayā sanandayā_
_Yutaṁ sukhaika-dāyakaṁ namāmi gopa-nāyakam ||3||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Kadamba-sūna-kuṇḍalaṁ**: Wearing flowers of the Kadamba tree as earrings.
- **Sucāru-gaṇḍa-maṇḍalaṁ**: With very beautiful/smooth cheeks.
- **Vraja-aṅganā-eka-vallabhaṁ**: The sole beloved of the women of Vraja.
- **Namāmi Kṛṣṇa-durlabham**: I bow to Krishna, who is unattainable (to the agitated mind).
- **Yaśodayā samodayā**: With Yashoda and with joy.
- **Sa-gopayā sa-nandayā**: With the Gopis and with Nanda.
- **Yutaṁ**: United/Surrounded.
- **Sukha-eka-dāyakaṁ**: The giver of unique/supreme happiness.
- **Namāmi Gopa-nāyakam**: I bow to the leader of the Gopas.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to Krishna, who wears Kadamba flowers as earrings and has beautiful cheeks. He is the sole beloved of the women of Vraja, yet He is hard to attain (for the arrogant). He is always surrounded by Yashoda, Nanda, and the Gopis in joy. He is the giver of supreme happiness and the leader of the Cowherds.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं उन कृष्ण को नमन करता हूँ, जिन्होंने कदम्ब के फूलों के कुंडल पहने हैं और जिनके गाल अत्यंत सुंदर हैं। वे व्रज की गोपियों के एकमात्र प्रेमी हैं, फिर भी (भक्तिहीन के लिए) अत्यंत दुर्लभ हैं। जो आनंदित यशोदा, नन्द और गोपगणों से घिरे रहते हैं और एकमात्र सुख देने वाले हैं, उन गोप-नायक को मैं नमन करता हूँ।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Exclusivity in Devotion**.

- **Eka-vallabham**: The "sole" beloved. In spiritual life, the Focus must be single-pointed (_Ekagrata_).
- **Durlabham (Difficult to get)**: Reminds us not to take the Divine for granted. It requires total surrender.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Kadamba Tree**: The tree of love/romance.
- **Yashoda/Nanda**: Represent "Fame" (Yasha) and "Joy" (Nanda) that naturally accompany the Divine Presence.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Brahman and Maya**:

- Krishna is _Durlabha_ (Hard to get) because He is beyond the senses. But He is _Vallabha_ (Beloved) when approached through the heart (Bhakti). He is both Transcendent and Immanent.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Reflect on "Durlabham". If God is everywhere, why is He hard to find? Because the mind is cluttered. Visualize clearing the clutter to find the "Rare Gem".

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: Cheap grace (thinking God exists to serve my whims).
- **Truth Installed**: God is the Supreme Prize, attained only by the sincere.

### 10) Essence

He is the Lover of the simple, but the Impossible Goal of the arrogant.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Is Krishna my "sole" beloved, or one of many hobbies?
2. Do I treat spiritual peace as something "cheap" or "rare" (Durlabha)?
3. Does my presence bring joy (Samodaya) to those around me?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Single Choice"**: For the next hour, do everything for "The One" (Krishna/Consciousness). Eat for Him, walk for Him. Be the _Eka-vallabha_.

## Verse 4

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**सदैव पादपङ्कजं मदीयमानसे निजं**
**दधानमुत्तमालकं नमामि नन्दबालकम् ।**
**समस्तदोषशोषणं समस्तलोकपोषणं**
**समस्तगोपमानसं नमामि नन्दलालसम् ॥४॥**

_Sadaiva pāda-paṅkajaṁ madīya-mānase nijaṁ_
_Dadhānam-uttam-ālakaṁ namāmi Nanda-bālakam |_
_Samasta-doṣa-śoṣaṇaṁ samasta-loka-poṣaṇaṁ_
_Samasta-gopa-mānasaṁ namāmi Nanda-lālasam ||4||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Sadaiva**: Always.
- **Pāda-paṅkajaṁ**: Lotus feet.
- **Madīya-mānase**: In my mind.
- **Nijaṁ**: My own / Firmly.
- **Dadhānam**: Holding/Bearing.
- **Uttama-ālakaṁ**: Beautiful curly locks of hair.
- **Namāmi Nanda-bālakam**: I bow to the child of Nanda.
- **Samasta-doṣa-śoṣaṇaṁ**: Drying up/Destroying all faults/negativities.
- **Samasta-loka-poṣaṇaṁ**: Nourishing all the worlds.
- **Samasta-gopa-mānasaṁ**: The heart/mind of all the cowherds.
- **Namāmi Nanda-lālasam**: I bow to the darling son of Nanda (who plays freely).

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to the child of Nanda, whose lotus feet I always hold firmly in my mind, and who creates beauty with His curly locks of hair. He dries up all faults and nourishes the entire universe. He is the very heart of all the cowherds. I bow to the darling of Nanda.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं नन्द के उस बालक को नमन करता हूँ, जिनके चरण-कमल मेरे मन में सदा विराजमान हैं और जिनके मस्तक पर सुंदर (घुंघराले) बाल सुशोभित हैं। वे समस्त दोषों को सुखाने वाले (नष्ट करने वाले) और समस्त लोकों का पालन-पोषण करने वाले हैं। वे समस्त ग्वालों के प्राण/मन हैं। मैं नन्द के उस लाल (प्यारे) को नमन करता हूँ।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Retention** and **Purification**.

- **Samasta-dosa-shoshanam**: God-remembrance acts like the sun, "drying up" the puddles of dirty water (mental faults/complexes).
- **Madiya-manase (In my mind)**: Internalizing the external deity.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Lotus Feet in Mind**: Grounding the intellect in something higher.
- **Curly Hair (Alakam)**: The entangled nature of the universe, which looks beautiful on Him.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Sustainer (Sthiti-karta)**:

- He is _Loka-poshanam_ (Nourisher of worlds). The Consciousness provides the fuel/life-force for everything to exist.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Imagine your mind is a lake. Place the "Lotus Feet" of Krishna in the center. Watch how the heat from those feet evaporates (Shoshanam) the mud of anger and greed.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "My faults define me."
- **Truth Installed**: The Light of Awareness dries up all faults instantly.

### 10) Essence

Hold His feet in your mind; they will dry up your swamp of sins.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. What occupies my mind "Sadaiva" (always)? Worry or Wisdom?
2. Do I trust Life to nourish me (Loka-poshanam)?
3. Are my faults permanent, or just moisture waiting to be dried?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Mental Anchor"**: Whenever you feel a negative emotion today, visualize touching mental "Feet" inside your head. Say, "Dry this up."

## Verse 5

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**भुवो भरावतारकं भवाब्धिकर्णधारकं**
**यशोमतीकिशोरकं नमामि चित्तचोरकम् ।**
**दृगन्तकान्तभङ्गिनं सदासदालसङ्गिनं**
**दिने दिने नवं नवं नमामि नन्दसम्भवम् ॥५॥**

_Bhuvo bharāvatārakaṁ bhavābdhi-karṇa-dhārakaṁ_
_Yaśomatī-kiśorakaṁ namāmi citta-corakam |_
_Dṛganta-kānta-bhaṅginaṁ sadā-sadālasaṅginaṁ_
_Dine dine navaṁ navaṁ namāmi Nanda-sambhavam ||5||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Bhuvo bhara-avatārakaṁ**: Reliever of the burden of the earth.
- **Bhavābdhi-karṇa-dhārakaṁ**: The Captain/Helmsman of the ship crossing the ocean of Samsara.
- **Yaśomatī-kiśorakaṁ**: The adolescent son of Yashoda.
- **Namāmi Citta-corakam**: I bow to the stealer of the heart/mind.
- **Dṛganta-kānta-bhaṅginaṁ**: Making beautiful gestures with the corner of His eyes.
- **Sadā-sadā-alas-aṅginaṁ**: Always languid/relaxed in posture (or companion of the eternal and transient).
- **Dine dine navaṁ navaṁ**: New and fresh day by day.
- **Nanda-sambhavam**: Born of Nanda.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to the stealer of hearts (Chitta-chora), the son of Yashoda, who removes the burden of the earth and steers the boat across the ocean of worldly existence. He casts beautiful sidelong glances and is always wonderfully relaxed. He appears ever-new, day by day. I bow to the son of Nanda.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं चित्त को चुराने वाले (चित्तचोर) यशोदा के किशोर को नमन करता हूँ, जो पृथ्वी का भार उतारने वाले और संसार सागर से पार लगाने वाले कर्णधार (केवट) हैं। जिनके नेत्रों के कटाक्ष सुंदर हैं, जो सदा (रास-लीला के बाद) आलस्य भरे अंगों वाले प्रतीत होते हैं, और जो दिन-प्रतिदिन नित्य नूतन (नए) लगते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Novelty** and **Letting Go**.

- **Citta-corakam**: Why does He "steal" the mind? Because we are holding onto it too tight! He steals our worries, plans, and ego.
- **Dine dine navam navam**: The spiritual experience is never stale; it is fresh every moment.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Karna-dharakam (Helmsman)**: He steers your life. If you grab the wheel, you panic. If you let Him steer, you cruise.
- **Stealing**: The act of Grace where the Ego is removed without the seeker's permission.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Nitya-Nutana (Eternal Freshness)**:

- Brahman is never "old". Time affects objects, not the Subject. Enlightenment is experiencing the "Now" as freshly new.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Sit in a boat in your visualization. The ocean is rough/stormy. Hand the oars to Krishna. You sit back and watch. He is the _Karna-dharaka_.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "Life is boring/repetitive" or "I am in control."
- **Truth Installed**: Life is Ever-New (Navam Navam) when accepted with presence.

### 10) Essence

Let the Stealer of Hearts take your ego; He steers the boat best.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Does my spiritual practice feel "fresh" (Navam) or mechanical?
2. Have I handed over the "oars" of my life?
3. What is He trying to "steal" from me that I am fighting to keep?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"Fresh Eyes"**: Look at a familiar object (tree/cup). Look at it as if you have never seen it before. Find the "Navam" (Newness) in it.

## Verse 6

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**गुणाकरं सुखाकरं कृपाकरं कृपापरं**
**सुरद्विषन्निकन्दनं नमामि गोपनन्दनम् ।**
**नवीनगोपनागरं नवीनकेलिलम्पटं**
**नमामि मेघसुन्दरं तटित्प्रभालसत्पटम् ॥६॥**

_Guṇākaraṁ sukhākaraṁ kṛpākaraṁ kṛpāparaṁ_
_Suradviṣan-nikandanaṁ namāmi Gopa-nandanam |_
_Navīna-gopa-nāgaraṁ navīna-keli-lampaṭaṁ_
_Namāmi megha-sundaraṁ taṭit-prabhā-lasat-paṭam ||6||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Guṇākaraṁ**: The mine/source of good qualities.
- **Sukhākaraṁ**: The source of happiness.
- **Kṛpākaraṁ**: The doer of compassion.
- **Kṛpāparaṁ**: Beyond compassion (Limitless grace).
- **Sura-dviṣan-nikandanaṁ**: The destroyer of the enemies of the Devas (Demons).
- **Namāmi Gopa-nandanam**: I bow to the delight of the Gopas.
- **Navīna-gopa-nāgaraṁ**: The ever-new gallant hero of the cowherds.
- **Navīna-keli-lampaṭaṁ**: Eager/greedy for new playful sports (Lilas).
- **Megha-sundaraṁ**: Beautiful like a rain cloud.
- **Taṭit-prabhā-lasat-paṭam**: Wearing robes (yellow) that shine like lightning.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to the delight of the Gopas, who is the abode of all virtues, happiness, and compassion. He destroys the demons (enemies of gods). He is the fresh hero of the cowherds, always eager for new playful sports. He is beautiful like a dark rain cloud, and His yellow robes flash like lightning.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं गोप-नन्दन को नमन करता हूँ, जो गुणों और सुखों की खान हैं, जो कृपा करने वाले और कृपा से परे (अपार) हैं। जो देवताओं के शत्रुओं (असुरों) का नाश करने वाले हैं। जो नित्य-नवीन नागर (चतुर) और नई-नई लीलाओं के लिए लम्पट (उत्सुक) हैं। जो मेघ (बादल) के समान सुंदर हैं और जिनके वस्त्र बिजली के समान चमकते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Hope** and **Playfulness**.

- **Keli-lampatam**: "Greedy for play." Usually greed is bad, but God is greedy to play with His creation. This invites us to view life as a game (_Lila_), not a struggle.
- **Megha (Cloud)**: Symbolizes cooling relief from the heat of life.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Cloud & Lightning**: Krishna is the Dark Cloud (Infinite/Unmanifest Mystery), and His Peethambaram (Yellow Robe) is the Lightning (Manifest Consciousness/Energy). The combination suggests the dynamic union of Spirit and Matter.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Saguna Brahman**:

- He is _Gunakaram_ (Repository of qualities) yet the source. The formless takes form to interact with the devotee.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize a dark monsoon cloud approaching a parched land (your heart). See the lightning. Feel the rain of _Sukha_ (Happiness) falling on you.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: God is distant or angry.
- **Truth Installed**: God is a Compassionate Cloud waiting to rain bliss.

### 10) Essence

He is the Raincloud of Grace wearing the Lightning of Wisdom.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Do I see my life problems as "Keli" (Play) or punishment?
2. Am I "Greedy" for spiritual experiences?
3. Am I a "Sura-dvisan" (Hater of good) in any part of my behavior?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Lightning Flash"**: Wear something bright/yellow today. Let it remind you of the lightning amidst the dark clouds of daily routine. Smile like the lightning.

## Verse 7

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**समस्तगोपनन्दनं हृदम्बुजैकमोदनं**
**नमामि कुञ्जमध्यगं प्रसन्नभानुशोभनम् ।**
**निकामकामदायकं दृगन्तचारुसायकं**
**रसालवेणुगायकं नमामि कुञ्जनायकम् ॥७॥**

_Samasta-gopa-nandanaṁ hṛd-ambujaika-modanaṁ_
_Namāmi kuñja-madhyagaṁ prasanna-bhānu-śobhanam |_
_Nikāma-kāma-dāyakaṁ dṛganta-cāru-sāyakaṁ_
_Rasāla-veṇu-gāyakaṁ namāmi kuñja-nāyakam ||7||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Samasta-gopa-nandanaṁ**: Analysis (already covered).
- **Hṛd-ambujaika-modanaṁ**: The sole delighter of the heart-lotus.
- **Kuñja-madhya-gaṁ**: Standing in the middle of the flowering grove/bower.
- **Prasanna-bhānu-śobhanam**: Shining like the bright/pleasant sun.
- **Nikāma-kāma-dāyakaṁ**: Fulfilling all desires completely.
- **Dṛganta-cāru-sāyakaṁ**: Whose glances are like beautiful arrows.
- **Rasāla-veṇu-gāyakaṁ**: Player of juicy/nectarine music on the flute.
- **Namāmi Kuñja-nāyakam**: I bow to the Lord of the Bower/Garden.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to the Lord of the groves, who stands in the middle of the garden and shines like the pleasant sun. He delights the lotus of the heart and fulfills all desires completely. His glances are like beautiful arrows (piercing the heart with love), and He plays nectarine music on the flute.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं कुंज-नायक (वृंदावन के कुंजों के स्वामी) को नमन करता हूँ, जो समस्त गोपों को आनंदित करते हैं और हृदय-कमल को खिलाने वाले एकमात्र सूर्य हैं। जो कुंजों के मध्य में स्थित हैं और प्रखर सूर्य के समान शोभायमान हैं। जो समस्त कामनाओं को पूर्ण करने वाले हैं, जिनके नेत्र-कटाक्ष सुंदर बाणों के समान हैं और जो रसीली वेणु बजाते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Centering** and **Emotional Release**.

- **Kunja-madhya-gam**: Standing in the _center_. This is the Center of Being.
- **Arrows (Sayakam)**: Divine Love hurts the ego. It pierces the armor of indifference.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Heart Lotus (Hrid-ambuja)**: The spiritual heart. Only the "Sun" (Consciousness) can make the lotus bloom.
- **Arrows**: The glance of the Guru/God that kills the individual ego.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Purnatvam (Fullness)**:

- _Nikama-kama-dayakam_ means He fulfills desires so completely that no desire remains. He gives the "Desireless State" by fullness.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize a dark forest (your subconscious). In the center, there is a lit clearing (Kunja). Krishna stands there playing music. Walk towards the center. Let His arrow glance pierce you.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I need worldly things to be happy."
- **Truth Installed**: He is the fulfiller of the deepest desire (Self-realization).

### 10) Essence

Let His arrow of love pierce your heart; it opens the inner lotus.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Is my heart-lotus closed or open (flowering)?
2. Am I standing at the periphery or moving to the "Center" (Madhya-gam)?
3. Does His flute music sound "Rasala" (Sweet) or distant to me?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"Heart Bloom"**: Place hands on chest. Inhale, imagining a lotus opening. Exhale, feeling the warmth of the inner Sun.

## Verse 8

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**विदग्धगोपिकामनोमनोज्ञतल्पशायिनं**
**नमामि कुञ्जकानने प्रवृद्धवह्निपायिनम् ।**
**यदा तदा यथा तथा तथैव कृष्णसत्कथा**
**मया सदैव गीयतां तथा कृपा विधीयताम् ॥८॥**

_Vidagdha-gopikā-mano-manojña-talpa-śāyinaṁ_
_Namāmi kuñja-kānane pravṛddha-vahni-pāyinam |_
_Yadā tadā yathā tathā tathaiva Kṛṣṇa-satkathā_
_Mayā sadaiva gīyatāṁ tathā kṛpā vidhīyatām ||8||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Vidagdha-gopikā**: The skillful/wise Gopis (devotees).
- **Mano-manojña-talpa-śāyinaṁ**: Resting on the bed of their minds.
- **Namāmi Kuñja-kānane**: I bow (to Him) in the forest groves.
- **Pravṛddha-vahni-pāyinam**: The drinker of the raging forest fire.
- **Yadā tadā**: Whenever, then.
- **Yathā tathā**: However, in whatever way.
- **Tathaiva**: Similarly / In that very way.
- **Kṛṣṇa-satkathā**: The true/glorious stories of Krishna.
- **Mayā sadaiva gīyatāṁ**: May be sung by me always.
- **Tathā kṛpā vidhīyatām**: Please bestow such grace.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

I bow to Him who rests on the bed of the minds of the wise Gopis and who drank the raging forest fire in the groves of Vraja. O Lord, please bestow such grace that whenever, wherever, and however I may be, I may always sing the glorious stories of Krishna.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

मैं उन्हें नमन करता हूँ जो चतुर गोपियों के मन रूपी सुंदर शय्या पर शयन करते हैं, और जिन्होंने कुंज-वन में लगी भयंकर आग (दावानल) को पी लिया था। (हे प्रभो!) मुझ पर ऐसी कृपा कीजिये कि जब भी, जैसे भी, जहां भी रहूँ, मेरे द्वारा सदा श्री कृष्ण की सत्कथा गाई जाती रहे।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Total Surrender** and **Safety**.

- **Vahni-payinam**: Drinking the fire. Life brings "fires" (crises). Krishna swallows them.
- **Talpa-shayinam**: If your mind is clean, God rests in it.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Forest Fire (Davagni)**: Represents the "Tri-tapa" (Threefold suffering) of Samsara. Only the Divine can swallow this fire and save the individual.
- **Singing (Giyatam)**: Chant/Flow is the state of connection.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Transformation**:

- The fire of Samsara destroys the ignorant, but for the Knower (Krishna), it is just a drink. Awareness consumes experience without being burnt by it.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize a ring of fire around you (your problems). Call out to Krishna. Watch Him open His mouth and inhale the fire. You are safe.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "The fire of life will consume me."
- **Truth Installed**: Grace is stronger than any fire.

### 10) Essence

Make your mind His bed, and He will drink your burning troubles.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Is my mind soft enough to be His "bed" or is it rocky?
2. Do I trust Him to drink the fires (crises) of my life?
3. Is my prayer simply to "remember Him always" (Sadaiva Giyatam)?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Fire Drink"**: When angry or stressed (internal fire), visualize offering that fire to the mouth of Krishna. "You drink this, I cannot handle it."

## Verse 9 (Phalashruti)

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**प्रमाणिकाष्टकद्वयं जपत्यधीत्य यः पुमान् ।**
**भवेत्स नन्दनन्दने भवे भवे सुभक्तिमान् ॥९॥**

_Pramāṇikāṣṭaka-dvayaṁ japaty-adhītya yaḥ pumān |_
_Bhavet-sa Nanda-nandane bhave bhave subhaktimān ||9||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Pramāṇikā-aṣṭaka-dvayaṁ**: These two eight-versed hymns (or the eight verses in Pramanika meter).
- **Japati adhītya**: Recites and studies.
- **Yaḥ pumān**: Whosoever (person).
- **Bhavet sa**: He becomes.
- **Nanda-nandane**: Towards the son of Nanda (Krishna).
- **Bhave bhave**: Birth after birth.
- **Su-bhaktimān**: Possessed of excellent/pure devotion.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Whoever recites and studies these eight verses composed in the Pramanika meter will attain pure and excellent devotion towards Lord Krishna (the son of Nanda) in every birth.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

जो भी मनुष्य 'प्रमाणिका' छंद में रचित इन दो अष्टकों (या इस अष्टक) का पाठ और अध्ययन करता है, वह जन्म-जन्मांतर में नन्दनन्दन श्री कृष्ण का परम भक्त बन जाता है।

### 5) Essence

The fruit of this prayer is **Devotion itself**, not material gain.
