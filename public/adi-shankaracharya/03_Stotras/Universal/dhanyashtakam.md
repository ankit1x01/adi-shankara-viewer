# Dhanyashtakam

**Theme**: The Blessed/Fortunate Ones (_Dhanya_)
**Author**: Adi Shankaracharya
**Focus**: Characteristics of the Realized Soul (Jivanmukta)

---

## Verse 1

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**तज्ज्ञानं प्रशमकरं यदिन्द्रियाणां**
**तज्ज्ञेयं यदुपनिषत्सु निश्चितार्थम् ।**
**ते धन्या भुवि परमार्थनिश्चितेहाः**
**शेषास्तु भ्रमनिलये परिभ्रमन्तः ॥ १ ॥**

_Taj-jñānaṁ praśama-karaṁ yad-indriyāṇāṁ_
_Taj-jñeyaṁ yad-upaniṣatsu niścitārtham |_
_Te dhanyā bhuvi paramārtha-niścitehāḥ_
_Śeṣāstu bhrama-nilaye paribhramantaḥ ||1||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Tat jñānaṁ**: That is (true) Knowledge.
- **Yad indriyāṇāṁ praśama-karaṁ**: Which causes the pacification/calming of the senses.
- **Tat jñeyaṁ**: That is the (true) Object of Knowledge.
- **Yad upaniṣatsu niścitārtham**: Which is the ascertained meaning/goal in the Upanishads.
- **Te dhanyā**: They are the blessed/fortunate ones.
- **Bhuvi**: On earth.
- **Paramārtha-niścitehāḥ**: Whose efforts/desires are fixed on the Supreme Truth.
- **Śeṣāstu**: The rest (others).
- **Bhrama-nilaye**: In the abode of delusion (Samsara).
- **Paribhramantaḥ**: Are wandering/revolving.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Only that is true Knowledge which calms the senses. Only that is the true Object of Knowledge which is asserted by the Upanishads. They actally are the blessed ones on earth whose efforts are directed solely toward the Supreme Truth. All others are merely wandering in the house of delusion.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

ज्ञान वही है जो इन्द्रियों को शांत कर दे। ज्ञेय (जानने योग्य) वही है जिसका निश्चय उपनिषदों में किया गया है। पृथ्वी पर वे ही 'धन्य' (भाग्यशाली) हैं जिनकी चेष्टा/इच्छा परमार्थ (परम सत्य) के लिए है। बाकी सब तो भ्रम (माया) के घर में भटक रहे हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Discernment** and **Focus**.

- Defines "Knowledge" not as information accumulation, but as _Prashamakaram_ (Peace-making). If your knowledge makes you anxious or arrogant, it is not _Jnana_.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Bhrama-nilaye (House of Delusion)**: The world of duality where we seek happiness outside.
- **Dhanya**: The true wealth is not money, but clarity.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Viveka (Discrimination)**:

- Distinguishing between _Vidya_ (Liberating Knowledge) and _Avidya_ (Binding Information).
- Connection: _Mundaka Upanishad_ distinction between _Para Vidya_ and _Apara Vidya_.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Checklist**: Before reading a book or watching a video, ask: "Will this calm my senses (_Prashamakaram_)?" If not, it is just noise.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I am smart because I know many things."
- **Truth Installed**: I am wise only if my senses are under control.

### 10) Essence

True knowledge brings peace; everything else is just wandering in confusion.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Does my current study/work bring me peace or agitation?
2. What is the "Object" I am trying to know? Is it the Self?
3. Am I "Dhanya" (Focused) or "Bhramanta" (Wandering)?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Peace Test"**: At the end of the day, review your inputs. Rate them 1-10 on "Peace Factor".

## Verse 2

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**आदौ विजित्य विषयान्मदमोहराग-**
**द्वेषादिशत्रुगणमाहृतयोगराज्याः ।**
**ज्ञात्वा मतं समनुभूय परात्मविद्या-**
**कान्तासुखं वनगृहे विचरन्ति धन्याः ॥ २ ॥**

_Ādau vijitya viṣayān-mada-moha-rāga-_
_Dveṣādi-śatru-gaṇam-āhṛta-yoga-rājyāḥ |_
_Jñātvā mataṁ samanubhūya parātma-vidyā-_
_Kāntā-sukhaṁ vana-gṛhe vicaranti dhanyāḥ ||2||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Ādau**: In the beginning/First.
- **Vijitya viṣayān**: Having conquered the sense-objects.
- **Mada-moha-rāga-dveṣa-ādi**: Pride, delusion, attachment, aversion, etc.
- **Śatru-gaṇam**: The host of enemies.
- **Āhṛta-yoga-rājyāḥ**: Having attained the kingdom of Yoga.
- **Jñātvā mataṁ**: Having known the Truth/View.
- **Samanubhūya**: Having fully experienced.
- **Parātma-vidyā-kāntā-sukhaṁ**: The joy of the beloved named "Knowledge of the Supreme Self".
- **Vana-gṛhe**: In the abode of the forest (solitude).
- **Vicaranti dhanyāḥ**: The blessed ones roam.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

First, having conquered the sense-objects and the inner enemies like pride, delusion, attachment, and aversion; having attained the Kingdom of Yoga; and having fully experienced the joy of the Beloved called "Self-Knowledge"—the blessed ones roam freely in the forest of solitude.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

पहले विषयों को और मद, मोह, राग, द्वेष आदि शत्रु वर्ग को जीतकर; जिन्होंने योग रूपी राज्य प्राप्त कर लिया है। फिर (ब्रह्म) मत को जानकर और 'परात्म-विद्या' रूपी प्रेयसी (कान्ता) के सुख का अनुभव करके, वे धन्य पुरुष वन रूपी घर में (स्वतंत्र) विचरण करते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Self-Mastery**.

- **Inner Enemies**: Identifies Pride, Delusion, Lust, Hate as the _real_ enemies, not people outside.
- **Yoga-Rajya**: Self-control is a "Kingdom". The Yogi is a King of himself.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Kanta (Beloved)**: Here, _Vidya_ (Knowledge) is the Beloved. The joy of intimacy with one's soul replaces the need for external romance.
- **Forest (Vana)**: State of mental solitude, not necessarily physical jungle.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Jivanmukti**:

- The verse describes the lifestyle of a realized sensation.
- **Krama-Mukti (Gradual)**: First conquer senses -> Then Yoga -> Then Knowledge -> Then Bliss.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize yourself as a King sitting on a throne. The "enemies" (Anger, Greed) are bowing down, conquered. You are holding hands with "Wisdom" (Kanta).

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "Freedom means doing whatever I want."
- **Truth Installed**: Freedom is ruling over your own impulses.

### 10) Essence

Conquer your inner enemies and rule the Kingdom of Yoga.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Who is ruling my life? Me or my "Mada-Moha" (Pride/Delusion)?
2. Have I experienced the "Romance" of Wisdom?
3. Can I find a "forest" (quiet space) in my city life?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Inner Throne"**: When tempted, say, "I am the King of this body. I do not bow to this desire."

## Verse 3

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**त्यक्त्वा गृहे रतिमधोगतिहेतुभूताम्**
**आत्मेच्छयोपनिषदर्थरसं पिबन्तः ।**
**वीतस्पृहा विषयभोगपदे विरक्ता**
**धन्याश्चरन्ति विजनेषु विरक्तसङ्गाः ॥ ३ ॥**

_Tyaktvā gṛhe ratim-adhogati-hetu-bhūtām_
_Ātmecchayopaniṣad-artha-rasaṁ pibantaḥ |_
_Vīta-spṛhā viṣaya-bhoga-pade viraktā_
_Dhanyāś-caranti vijaneṣu virakta-saṅgāḥ ||3||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Tyaktvā gṛhe ratim**: Having abandoned attachment to home/householder life.
- **Adhogati-hetu-bhūtām**: Which is the cause of downfall (spiritual stagnation).
- **Ātmecchayā**: By their own will/desire for the Self.
- **Upaniṣad-artha-rasaṁ pibantaḥ**: Drinking the nectar/juice of the meaning of Upanishads.
- **Vīta-spṛhā**: Free from cravings.
- **Viṣaya-bhoga-pade viraktā**: Dispassionate towards the abodes of sense-enjoyment.
- **Dhanyāś-caranti**: The blessed ones wander.
- **Vijaneṣu**: In solitary places.
- **Virakta-saṅgāḥ**: Detached from company.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Abandoning attachment to the home (worldly security), which causes downfall; drinking the nectar of the Upanishads by their own will; free from cravings and detached from positions of enjoyment; the blessed ones wander in solitude, free from all attachment to company.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

गृह-सक्ति (घर/परिवार का मोह) जो अधोगति का कारण है, उसे त्यागकर; अपनी इच्छा से उपनिषदों के अर्थ रूपी रस का पान करते हुए; इच्छाओं से मुक्त और भोग-स्थानों से विरक्त होकर; वे धन्य पुरुष एकांत (विजन) में विचरण करते हैं, जिनका संग (मोह) छूट चुका है।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Autonomy** and **Intellectual Delight**.

- **Drinking the Rasa**: Philosophy is usually seen as dry. Here, it is _Rasa_ (Juice/Nectar). The realized mind enjoys Truth like a drunkard enjoys wine.
- **Virakta-sanga**: Freedom from the need for social validation.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Griha (Home)**: Symbolizes the "Comfort Zone" or stagnation.
- **Vijane (Solitude)**: The space where only the One exists.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Sannyasa (Renunciation)**:

- Realising that worldly attachment (_Rati_) leads to _Adhogati_ (Lower birth/suffering).
- **Jnana-Yajna**: Feeding the fire of Self with the ghee of Upanishadic thought.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Imagine "Loneliness" turning into "Solitude". In Loneliness, you miss others. In Solitude, you enjoy the _Rasa_ of the Self.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I am safe in my comfort zone."
- **Truth Installed**: Real safety lies in the freedom of the Spirit.

### 10) Essence

Drink the nectar of Wisdom and walk alone in freedom.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Is my attachment to "home/comfort" stopping my growth?
2. Do I find "Jucie" (Rasa) in wisdom, or is it boring?
3. Can I be happy without anyone around me?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Solo Walk"**: Take a long walk alone without a phone. Drink the "Rasa" of your own existence.

## Verse 4

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**त्यक्त्वा ममाहमिति बन्धकरे पदे द्वे**
**मानावमानसदृशाः समदर्शिनश्च ।**
**कर्तारमन्यमवगम्य तदर्पितानि**
**कुर्वन्ति कर्मपरिपाकफलानि धन्याः ॥ ४ ॥**

_Tyaktvā mamāham-iti bandha-kare pade dve_
_Mānāvamāna-sadṛśāḥ sama-darśinaś-ca |_
_Kartāram-anyam-avagamya tad-arpitāni_
_Kurvanti karma-paripāka-phalāni dhanyāḥ ||4||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Tyaktvā mama-aham-iti**: Having abandoned "Mine" and "I".
- **Bandha-kare pade dve**: These two words that cause bondage.
- **Māna-avamāna-sadṛśāḥ**: Equal in honor and dishonor.
- **Sama-darśinaś-ca**: And seeing sameness everywhere.
- **Kartāram-anyam-avagamya**: Understanding the Doer to be "Other" (God/Prakriti).
- **Tad-arpitāni**: Offering actions to Him.
- **Kurvanti karma-paripāka-phalāni**: They go through the fruits of past actions (Prarabdha).
- **Dhanyāḥ**: The blessed ones.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Abandoing the two binding words "I" and "Mine"; remaining equal in honor and dishonor and seeing the same Self everywhere; understanding that the true Doer is "Other" (the Divine) and offering all actions to Him; the blessed ones simply perform/experience the fruits of their past karma without attachment.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

बंधान करने वाले दो शब्दों- 'मैं' और 'मेरा' को त्यागकर; मान और अपमान में समान रहने वाले और समदर्शी; कर्ता को (स्वयं से) 'अन्य' (ईश्वर/प्रकृति) समझकर; सब कर्म उसे समर्पित करते हुए; वे धन्य पुरुष केवल प्रारब्ध कर्मों के फलों को भोगते (या पूरा करते) हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Equanimity**.

- **Mama/Aham**: The root of all neurosis. "My" car, "My" insult. Dropping these allows the mind to float.
- **Mana-Avamana**: Immunity to public opinion.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Two Words (Pade Dve)**: The binary code of Samsara (I and Mine).
- **Other Doer**: Recognizing the cosmic mechanism (_Prakriti_) runs the show, not the ego.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Karma Yoga & Sakshi Bhava**:

- **Kartaram Anyam**: "Prakriti kriya maanaani gunaih..." (Gita). Qualities act on qualities; the Self is silent.
- **Prarabdha**: The body continues to act due to momentum, but the "Driver" has stepped out.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Sit and watch your breath. Say "I am not breathing. Breath is happening." Watch thoughts. "I am not thinking. Thoughts are happening." Disown the doership.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I am the doer."
- **Truth Installed**: The Universal Power acts; I am the Witness.

### 10) Essence

Drop "I" and "Mine"—the only two chains holding you down.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. How many times today did I say/think "Mine"?
2. Am I disturbed by an insult (Avamana)?
3. Can I act without thinking "I did this"?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Proxy Actor"**: For one hour, imagine you are an actor played by God. Everything you do is His script. You have no responsibility, just perform.

## Verse 5

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**त्यक्त्वैषणात्रयमवेक्षितमोक्षमार्गा**
**भैक्षामृतेन परिकल्पितदेहयात्राः ।**
**ज्योतिः परात्परतरं परमात्मसंज्ञं**
**धन्या द्विजा रहसि हृद्यवलोकयन्ति ॥ ५ ॥**

_Tyaktvaiṣaṇā-trayam-avekṣita-mokṣa-mārgā_
_Bhaikṣāmṛtena parikalpita-deha-yātrāḥ |_
_Jyotiḥ parāt-parataraṁ paramātma-sañjñaṁ_
_Dhanyā dvijā rahasi hṛdy-avalokayanti ||5||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Tyaktvā-eṣaṇā-trayam**: Having abandoned the three desires (Son, Wealth, Worlds).
- **Avekṣita-mokṣa-mārgā**: Seeking only the path of Liberation.
- **Bhaikṣā-amṛtena**: With the nectar of alms (begged food).
- **Parikalpita-deha-yātrāḥ**: Sustaining the journey of the body.
- **Jyotiḥ parāt-parataraṁ**: The Light which is higher than the highest.
- **Paramātma-sañjñaṁ**: Known as the Supreme Self.
- **Dhanyā dvijā**: The blessed twice-born (sages).
- **Rahasi hṛdi**: In the secret place of the heart.
- **Avalokayanti**: They perceive/see.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Abandoning the three desires (for offspring, wealth, and worlds), focused only on the path of liberation, sustaining their body with the nectar of alms; the blessed sages perceive in the secret cave of their heart that Supreme Light which is higher than the highest, known as the Paramatman.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

तीन एषणाओं (पुत्र, वित्त, लोक की इच्छा) को त्यागकर; मोक्ष मार्ग पर दृष्टि रखकर; भिक्षा रूपी अमृत से शरीर की यात्रा (जीवन) चलाते हुए; वे धन्य द्विज (ज्ञानी) हृदय के एकांत (रहस्य) में उस 'परमात्म' नामक परम-ज्योति का दर्शन करते हैं जो श्रेष्ठ से भी श्रेष्ठ है।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Simplicity** and **Inner Vision**.

- **Eshana Trayam**: The biological and social drives that keep us busy. Dropping them frees up massive energy.
- **Bhaiksha (Alms)**: Living on whatever comes by chance, accepting life's offering without demand.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Jyoti (Light)**: The Light of Awareness that illuminates all thoughts.
- **Rahasi (Secret)**: The Truth is hidden in plain sight, in the "Cave" of the heart.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Self-Realization**:

- **Dvijah**: Means "Twice-born". First birth from womb, second birth from Wisdom (Gayatri/Brahma-Vidya).
- **Parat-parataram**: Beyond the Gross and Subtle, the Causal and the Witness.

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Visualize a small flame in the center of your chest. This flame needs no fuel. It is the witness of the darkness. Watch it steadily (_Avalokayanti_).

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "I need family/money/fame to be complete."
- **Truth Installed**: The Light of Fullness is already within.

### 10) Essence

Survive on little, desire nothing, and watch the Light within.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Which of the 3 desires (Money, Relations, Status) binds me most?
2. Can I simplify my "physical journey" (Deha Yatra)?
3. Have I located the "secret cave" in my heart?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Minimalist Meal"**: Eat one meal today that is very simple (like alms). Feel the gratitude for sustenance, not taste.

## Verse 6

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**नासन्न सन्न सदसन्न महसन्नचाणु**
**न स्त्री पुमान्न च नपुंसकमेकबीजम् ।**
**यैर्ब्रह्म तत्सममुपासितमेकचित्तैः**
**धन्या विरेजुरितरे भवपाशबद्धाः ॥ ६ ॥**

_Nāsanna sanna sadasanna mahasanna cāṇu_
_Na strī pumānna ca napuṁsakam-eka-bījam |_
_Yair-brahma tat-samam-upāsitam-eka-cittaiḥ_
_Dhanyā virejur-itare bhava-pāśa-baddhāḥ ||6||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Na asat na sat**: Neither non-existent nor existent (as an object).
- **Na sad-asat**: Nor both existent and non-existent.
- **Na mahat na ca aṇu**: Neither big nor small.
- **Na strī pumān na ca napuṁsakam**: Neither female, male, nor neuter.
- **Eka-bījam**: The One Seed (of all).
- **Yaiḥ brahma tat**: By whom that Brahman...
- **Samam upāsitam**: Is worshipped as the Same/Equal (essence).
- **Eka-cittaiḥ**: With one-pointed mind.
- **Dhanyā virejuḥ**: Those blessed ones shine glorious.
- **Itare bhava-pāśa-baddhāḥ**: Others are bound by the ropes of Samsara.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

That which is neither existent (as an object) nor non-existent, nor both; neither great nor small; neither female nor male nor neuter; the One Seed of all. Those blessed ones who worship/meditate on that Brahman as the "Samam" (Equal Reality) with a one-pointed mind—they shine gloriously. All others are bound by the ropes of birth and death.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

जो न असत् है न सत्, न दोनों (सदसत्); न महान (बड़ा) है न अणु (छोटा); न स्त्री है, न पुरुष, न नपुंसक; जो सबका एक-मात्र बीज (कारण) है। वे धन्य पुरुष सुशोभित होते हैं जिन्होंने एकाग्र-चित्त होकर उस 'सम' (सर्वत्र समान) ब्रह्म की उपासना की है। बाकी सब तो संसार-पाश में बंधे हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Transcending Categories**.

- The mind thinks in categories (Gender, Size, Existence). The Truth is _Neti-Neti_ (Not this, not that).
- **Samam**: Seeing the underlying Equality evokes a state of mental balance.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Seed (Bija)**: Only the Seed is real; the tree is just an expression.
- **Ropes (Pasha)**: Our concepts and labels are the ropes that tie us.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Nirguna Brahman**:

- Reference to _Shvetashvatara Upanishad_ and _Mandukya Karika_.
- Breaking the logical constructs to arrive at the Direct Experience (_Aparoksha Anubhuti_).

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: Think of yourself. Are you male/female? That is the body. Are you big/small? That is form. Go to the "Seed" awareness which has no labels. Stay there.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: Defined Identity (I am a man, I am small, etc.).
- **Truth Installed**: Undefined Be-ness.

### 10) Essence

The Truth has no gender, size, or form; be That and shine.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Am I attached to my gender identity?
2. Do I judge things by size/status?
3. Can I focus my mind (_Eka-citta_) on the Abstract?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Label Drop"**: Look at people today. Mentally strip them of gender, age, and status. See them as "Life Energy".

## Verse 7

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**अज्ञानपङ्कपरिमग्नमपेतसारं**
**दुःखालयं मरणजन्मजरावसक्तम् ।**
**संसारबन्धनमनित्यमवेक्ष्य धन्या**
**ज्ञानासिना तदवशीर्य विनिश्चयन्ति ॥ ७ ॥**

_Ajñāna-paṅka-parimagnam-apeta-sāraṁ_
_Duḥkhālayaṁ maraṇa-janma-jarāvasaktam |_
_Saṁsāra-bandhanam-anityam-avekṣya dhanyā_
_Jñānāsinā tad-avaśīrya viniścayanti ||7||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Ajñāna-paṅka-parimagnam**: Sunk deep in the mud of ignorance.
- **Apeta-sāraṁ**: Devoid of essence/worthless.
- **Duḥkhālayaṁ**: The abode of sorrow.
- **Maraṇa-janma-jarā-avasaktam**: Filled with death, birth, and old age.
- **Saṁsāra-bandhanam anityam**: The bondage of Samsara is impermanent/temporary.
- **Avekṣya**: Having observed/realized this.
- **Dhanyā jñānāsinā**: The blessed ones, with the sword of Knowledge.
- **Tad-avaśīrya**: Having cut/shattered it.
- **Viniścayanti**: They attain firm resolve/freedom.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

Realizing that this bondage of Samsara is sunk in the mud of ignorance, is devoid of essence, is the abode of sorrow, filled with birth, death, and old age, and is impermanent; the blessed ones cut through it with the Sword of Knowledge and attain firm realization.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

यह देखकर कि यह संसार-बंधन अज्ञान रूपी कीचड़ में डूबा है, सार-हीन है, दुखों का घर है, और जन्म-मृत्यु-बुढ़ापे से ग्रस्त है; धन्य पुरुष 'ज्ञान रूपी तलवार (असि)' से इस अनित्य बंधन को काट डालते हैं और (स्वरूप का) निश्चय कर लेते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Disillusionment** and **Courage**.

- **Apeta-saram**: Recognizing that the world has no intrinsic content; we project value onto it.
- **Sword (Asina)**: Knowledge is not soft; it is a weapon. It destroys the false.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Mud (Panka)**: Ignorance is sticky and dirty.
- **Sword**: Discrimination (_Viveka_).

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Vairagya (Dispassion)**:

- Seeing the _Dosha_ (Defects) in Samsara (Birth, Death, Pain) is the first step to freedom (_Gita Ch 13_).

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Visualisation**: Image your attachment to the world as a rope. Imagine a shining sword of light in your hand. Slash the rope. Feel the snap.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "The world will make me happy."
- **Truth Installed**: The world is a mud-pit; Happiness is within.

### 10) Essence

Cut the sticky mud of Samsara with the Sword of Truth.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Am I stuck in the "Mud" (Ignorance) or standing on solid ground?
2. Do I see the "Duḥkhalayam" (Sorrow-nature) of worldly pursuits?
3. Is my Knowledge sharp like a sword?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Reality Check"**: When excited about a worldly thing, whisper: "Maraṇa-Janma-Jara" (Death, Birth, Old age). It balances the infatuation.

## Verse 8

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**शान्तैरनन्यमतिभिर्मधुरस्वभावैः**
**एकत्वनिश्चितमनोभिरपेतमोहैः ।**
**साकं वनेषु विजितात्मपदस्वरूपं**
**तद्वस्तु सम्यगनिशं विमृशन्ति धन्याः ॥ ८ ॥**

_Śāntair-ananya-matibhir-madhura-svabhāvaiḥ_
_Ekatva-niścita-manobhir-apeta-mohaiḥ |_
_Sākaṁ vaneṣu vijitātma-pada-svarūpaṁ_
_Tad-vastu samyag-aniśaṁ vimṛśanti dhanyāḥ ||8||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Śāntaiḥ**: With the peaceful ones.
- **Ananya-matibhiḥ**: With those of exclusive/focused minds.
- **Madhura-svabhāvaiḥ**: With those of sweet nature.
- **Ekatva-niścita-manobhiḥ**: With those whose minds are resolved in Oneness.
- **Apeta-mohaiḥ**: With those free from delusion.
- **Sākaṁ**: Together (in company of).
- **Vaneṣu**: In forest/retreats.
- **Vijita-ātma-pada-svarūpaṁ**: The nature of the Self-state which is conquered/realized.
- **Tad-vastu**: That Reality (Thing).
- **Samyag-aniśaṁ vimṛśanti**: They contemplate deeply and constantly (day and night).
- **Dhanyāḥ**: The blessed ones.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

In the company of peaceful, sweet-natured sages who are free from delusion and whose minds are resolved in Oneness; the blessed ones constantly and deeply contemplate on that Supreme Reality (Tad Vastu) and the Nature of the Self in the forests of solitude.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

शांत, अनन्य बुद्धि वाले, मधुर स्वभाव वाले, एकत्व के निश्चय वाले और मोह-रहित संतों के 'साथ' वनों में (एकांत में); वे धन्य पुरुष दिन-रात 'उस वस्तु' (परम सत्य) और आत्म-पद के स्वरूप का सम्यक विचार (चिंतन) करते हैं।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

Trains **Satsang** (Holy Association).

- **Madhura-svabhava**: A Yogi is not rude or arrogant. He is "Sweet-natured".
- **Vimrishanti**: Active contemplation, not just passive sitting.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Tad Vastu (That Thing)**: The Ultimate is often called "Thing" because it is the only Substantial Reality.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Nididhyasana (Contemplation)**:

- Surrounded by like-minded seekers (_Satsang_), reinforcing the Non-dual Truth day and night (_Anisham_).

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Action**: Find a "Buddy" or a community that is _Shanta_ and _Madhura_. Discuss "That Thing" (Truth) with them, rather than gossip.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "Spiritual people are boring/grumpy."
- **Truth Installed**: True spirituality is Sweet and Peaceful.

### 10) Essence

Keep company with the Sweet and Wise, and talk only of the Truth.

### 11) Reflection Questions

1. Are my friends "Madhura-svabhava" (Sweet) or toxic?
2. Do we discuss "Ekatva" (Oneness) or politics?
3. Am I reflecting on the Truth "Anisham" (constantly)?

### 12) Practical Exercise

**"The Sweet Speech"**: Today, speak only when necessary, and speak sweetly. Be a _Madhura-svabhava_ person.

## Verse 9 (Result/Phalashruti)

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**अहिमिव जनयोगं सर्वदा वर्जयेद्यः**
**कुणपमिव सुनारीं त्यक्तुकामो विरागी ।**
**विषमिव विषयान् यो मन्यमानो दुरन्तान्**
**जयति परमहंसो मुक्तिभावं समेति ॥ ९ ॥**

_Ahim-iva jana-yogaṁ sarvadā varjayed-yaḥ_
_Kuṇapam-iva sunārīṁ tyaktukāmo virāgī |_
_Viṣam-iva viṣayān yo manyamāno durantān_
_Jayati paramahaṁso mukti-bhāvaṁ sameti ||9||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Ahim-iva**: Like a snake.
- **Jana-yogaṁ**: The crowd/company of people.
- **Sarvadā varjayed-yaḥ**: Who avoids always.
- **Kuṇapam-iva**: Like a corpse.
- **Sunārīṁ**: A beautiful woman (object of lust).
- **Tyaktu-kāmo**: Desiring to abandon.
- **Virāgī**: The dispassionate one.
- **Viṣam-iva**: Like poison.
- **Viṣayān**: Sense-objects.
- **Manyamāno durantān**: Considering them as leading to bad ends.
- **Jayati Paramahaṁso**: That Supreme Swan (Paramahamsa) triumphs.
- **Mukti-bhāvaṁ sameti**: And attains the state of Liberation.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

The Dispassionate One who avoids crowds like a snake, who rejects objects of lust (like a beautiful woman/man) as if they were a corpse, and who considers sense-objects to be fatal like poison—that Paramahamsa (Supreme Swan) triumphs and attains the state of Liberation.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

जो भीड़ (लोगों के संग) को सांप की तरह सदा त्याग देता है; जो सुंदरी (या काम-भोग) को शव (लाश) की तरह त्याग देता है; जो विषयों को हलाहल विष की तरह घातक मानता है; वह वैरागी 'परमहंस' विजय प्राप्त करता है और मुक्ति पद को पाता है।

### 5) PSYCHOLOGICAL Layer

**Extreme Detox**.

- To break strong addictions (Samsara), strong antidotes are needed.
- **Snake/Corpse/Poison**: These strong negative visualizations help break the magnetic pull of lust and social validation.

### 6) SYMBOLIC Layer

- **Paramahamsa (Great Swan)**: The bird that can separate milk from water (Truth from Falsehood).
- **Snake**: Danger to peace.

### 7) VEDANTIC Layer

**Tivra Vairagya** (Intense Dispassion).

- This is not for beginners, but for the one ready for the final leap (_Mukti_).

### 8) SADHANA Layer

**Meditation**: When a distraction arises, label it "Poison". It stops the mind from engaging.

### 9) Illusion vs. Truth

- **Illusion Dissolved**: "Crowds and pleasures are life."
- **Truth Installed**: Solitude and Self-control are Life.

### 10) Essence

Avoid distraction like poison; attain the Triumph of the Spirit.

## Verse 10 (Conclusion - The Vision)

### 1) Original Sanskrit & Transliteration

**सम्पूर्‍णं जगदेव नन्दनवनं सर्वेऽपि कल्पद्रुमा**
**गांगं वारि समस्तवारिनिवहः पुण्याः समस्ताः क्रियाः ।**
**वाचः प्राकृतसंस्कृताः श्रुतिशिरो वाराणसी मेदिनी**
**सर्वावस्थितिरस्य वस्तुविषया दृष्टे परब्रह्मणि ॥ १० ॥**

_Sampūrṇaṁ jagad-eva nandana-vanaṁ sarve'pi kalpa-drumā_
_Gāṅgaṁ vāri samasta-vāri-nivahaḥ puṇyāḥ samastāḥ kriyāḥ |_
_Vācaḥ prākṛta-saṁskṛtāḥ śruti-śiro Vārāṇasī medinī_
_Sarvāvasthitirasya vastu-viṣayā dṛṣṭe Para-brahmaṇi ||10||_

### 2) Word-by-Word Meaning

- **Dṛṣṭe Para-brahmaṇi**: When the Supreme Brahman is realized/seen (by the Dhanya).
- **Sampūrṇaṁ jagad-eva nandana-vanaṁ**: The whole world becomes the Garden of Heaven (Nandana).
- **Sarve api kalpa-drumā**: All trees become wish-fulfilling trees.
- **Samasta-vāri-nivahaḥ**: All water bodies become.
- **Gāṅgaṁ vāri**: The holy Ganga water.
- **Samastāḥ kriyāḥ**: All actions become.
- **Puṇyāḥ**: Holy/Meritorious.
- **Vācaḥ prākṛta-saṁskṛtāḥ**: All speech (vulgar or refined) becomes.
- **Śruti-śiro**: The Upanishads (Veda-Anta).
- **Medinī**: The whole earth becomes.
- **Vārāṇasī**: The holy city of Kashi.
- **Sarva-avasthitiḥ**: His every state/movement.
- **Vastu-viṣayā**: Is an offering/meditation on the Reality.

### 3) Simple English Meaning

When the Supreme Brahman is realized, everything changes for that blessed one: The whole world becomes the Garden of Heaven; all trees are wish-fulfilling trees; all water is the holy Ganga; all actions are holy rites; all speech (simple or refined) is the scripture; the whole earth is the holy city of Varanasi; and every state of existence is a meditation on the Reality.

### 4) Simple Hindi Meaning

परब्रह्म का दर्शन हो जाने पर (ज्ञानी के लिए): पूरा जगत ही नंदन-वन (स्वर्ग) बन जाता है; सभी वृक्ष कल्पवृक्ष हो जाते हैं; सारा जल गंगाजल हो जाता है; सारी क्रियाएं पवित्र (पूजा) बन जाती हैं; (साधारण) बोली भी उपनिषद बन जाती है; पूरी धरती ही काशी (वाराणसी) हो जाती है। उसकी हर स्थिति केवल 'वस्तु' (ब्रह्म) का ही विलास होती है।

### 5) Essence

**Total Transformation**. The world doesn't change physically, but the **Vision** transforms it into Divine Perfection. This is the state of the _Dhanya_ (Blessed One).
